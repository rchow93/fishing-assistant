## Fishing Intelligence  

### Bite Status (Recent)  
- **Active Species**: Rockfish, Lingcod, Dungeness Crab, Monkeyface Eel, Stripers.  
- **Bite Intensity**: Moderate to strong during high/low tide transitions.  

### Prime Locations & Depths  
| Area               | Structure / Feature         | Typical Depth (ft) |  
|--------------------|----------------------------|--------------------|  
| Pillar Point Harbor | Rocky outcrops, piers       | 30-50              |

| Deep Reefs         | Steep walls, kelp beds     | 50-100             |  
| Tidal Pools        | Shallow flats, crevices    | 2-5                |  

### Bait Recommendations  
- **Primary Bait**: Squid, anchovies, and rock crab (for crab-focused trips).  
- **Secondary Bait**: Dungeness crab (for rockfish) and shrimp (for stripers).  

### Rigging & Tackle Specs  
| Item               | Recommended Specification                      |  
|--------------------|------------------------------------------------|  
| Line              | 20-30 lb test (deep reefs), 15-20 lb (shallow) |  
| Hook              | 1/0 to 3/0 circle hooks (rockfish), 2/0 (crab) |  
| Reel              | Spinning or baitcasting with 100-200 yards line |  
| Leader            | 10-15 lb fluorocarbon (for deep water)         |  

### Fishing Techniques  
- Cast near rocky structures, piers, and kelp beds during high/low tide transitions.  
- Use heavy tackle for deep reefs (e.g., 20-30 lb test line) and light tackle for tidal pools.  
- For rock crab, deploy hoop nets or snares in tidal pools during high tide.  
- Retrieve baits slowly to mimic natural movement.  

### Current Regulations  
- **Depth Restrictions**: No depth limits for rockfishing (effective Sept 18, 2025).  
- **Seasonal Closure**: Salmon fishing closed; rock crab season open year-round via hoop nets/snares.  
- **Bag Limits**: Check local guidelines for rockfish (no daily limit for non-residents).  

## Safety Tips & Practical Advice  
- Avoid fishing during low tide (03:09 AM and 15:58 PM) when fish are less active.  
- Use waterproof gear and thermal clothing due to cold water temperatures (13.7°C-13.8°C).  
- Exercise caution with small vessels during moderate wave heights (2.3m-2.6m).  
- Monitor tides and currents, as full moon increases tidal strength and may affect navigation.
