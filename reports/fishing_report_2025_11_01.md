## Fishing Intelligence  

### Bite Status (Recent)  
- **Active feeding** during high tides (08:08 and 19:54) and full moon phases.  
- **Best times**: 1–2 hours before/after high tide (approx. 07:00 AM and 18:54 PM).  

### Prime Locations & Depths  
| Area | Structure / Feature | Typical Depth (ft) |  
|------|---------------------|--------------------|  
| Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary | Rocky outcrops, kelp beds | 80–150 |  
| Piers & Jetties | Artificial structures, tidal pools | 50–100 |  
| Deep-Sea Areas | Canyon walls, underwater ridges | 150–300 |  

### Bait Recommendations  
- **Natural baits**: Squid, anchovies, herring, live sardines.  
- **Artificial lures**: Small jigs (1/8 oz), soft plastics, or lures mimicking squid.  

### Rigging & Tackle Specs  
| Item | Recommended Specification |  
|------|---------------------------|  
| Line | 20–30 lb test monofilament or fluorocarbon |  
| Weight | 1/16 oz to 1/4 oz for deeper areas; 1/8 oz for shallow |  
| Hook Size | 1/0 to 2/0 circle hooks (for rockfish) |  
| Reel | Spinning or baitcasting with 3000–5000 drag capacity |  

### Fishing Techniques  
- **Slow presentations** with weighted rigs for deeper areas (150–300 ft).  
- **Shallow rigs** (50–100 ft) during ebbing tides near piers/jetties.  
- **Vertical jigging** with small lures (e.g., 1/8 oz jigs) around structures.  
- **Use sonar** to locate schools near drop-offs or rocky outcrops.  

### Current Regulations  
- **Season**: Check California Department of Fish and Wildlife (CDFW) for seasonal closures (e.g., rockfish season runs Oct–May in some zones).  
- **Size Limits**: Minimum size varies by species (e.g., rockfish ≥ 12–14 inches).  
- **Gear Restrictions**: No lead weights in marine reserves; use barbless hooks.  

---

## Safety Tips & Practical Advice  
- **Layer clothing** for cool temperatures (14.5°C) and potential hypothermia risks.  
- **Avoid midday sun**; fish prefer cooler waters (14–16°C).  
- **Monitor tides** and currents, especially during full moon phases.  
- **Use life jackets** and stay hydrated.
